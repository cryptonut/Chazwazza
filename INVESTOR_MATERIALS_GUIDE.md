## ğŸ“Š Pitch Deck Structure

### The 12-Slide Framework

| Slide | Title | Content | Time |
|-------|-------|---------|------|
| 1 | Cover | Company name, tagline, logo | 15s |
| 2 | Problem | Pain point you're solving | 45s |
| 3 | Solution | Your product/approach | 45s |
| 4 | Market | TAM/SAM/SOM analysis | 30s |
| 5 | Product | Screenshots, demo highlights | 60s |
| 6 | Traction | Metrics, milestones, proof | 45s |
| 7 | Business Model | How you make money | 30s |
| 8 | Competition | Your differentiation | 30s |
| 9 | Team | Founders and key hires | 30s |
| 10 | Financials | Projections summary | 30s |
| 11 | The Ask | Raise amount, use of funds | 30s |
| 12 | Close | Contact info, next steps | 15s |

**Total: ~8 minutes** (leaves time for questions in a 15-min meeting)

## ğŸš« Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Inconsistent numbers | Destroys credibility | Single source of truth |
| Too many slides | Loses attention | 12 slides max |
| No clear ask | Wastes everyone's time | Specific amount and terms |
| Generic outreach | Gets ignored | Personalize every email |
| Missing assumptions | Can't validate model | Document everything |
| Outdated materials | Looks sloppy | Review before each meeting |
